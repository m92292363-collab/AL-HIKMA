The President's Message

Welcome Message

In the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

It is with great honour and deep gratitude that I welcome you to the official platform of the Gambia Islamic Union (GIU), an institution that, since 1963, has stood as a beacon of authentic Islamic education, social service, and community development in The Gambia and beyond.

Our journey began with a noble vision: to spread knowledge rooted in the Qur’an and Sunnah, uplift communities, and empower generations with both faith and skills. Today, by Allah’s grace, that vision continues to flourish through our extensive network of schools, Al-Hikmah University, humanitarian programmes, and da’wah initiatives that reach every corner of our beloved nation.

As President, I am committed to preserving GIU’s founding values while steering the Union toward greater achievements—expanding access to education, strengthening partnerships, and enhancing our role as a trusted guardian of Islamic identity and social progress.

I invite you to explore our work, engage with our programmes, and join us in this sacred mission of service to Allah and humanity. Together, let us continue to build a stronger, more united, and more enlightened Ummah.

May Allah bless our efforts and grant success to us all.

Dr. Mbye Kebba Kah
The President
Who We Are

The Gambia Islamic Union (GIU)

Established in 1963, the Gambia Islamic Union is one of The Gambia’s pioneering Islamic educational and charitable institutions. Rooted in authentic Islamic teachings and committed to social development, GIU offers education, da’wah, humanitarian aid, and capacity-building programs across The Gambia.

Our Vision & Mission

The Gambia Islamic Union envisions a society enriched with knowledge, faith, and unity. Guided by the Qur’an and Sunnah, we are committed to providing quality education, impactful humanitarian services, and meaningful da’wah initiatives that nurture moral excellence, empower communities, and promote socio-economic progress for The Gambia and the wider Ummah.
